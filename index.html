Predominantly α-helical region. The O-helix undergoes a 28°/19 Å conformational closure upon dNTP binding (open → ajar → closed; PDB 9F6D/9F6E/9F6F), ensuring Watson–Crick base-pair geometry. Rate-limiting step for nucleotide incorporation and the structural basis of replicative fidelity.

Helical bundle that grips the minor groove of the nascent duplex DNA product. Contacts extend ~8 bp into the product duplex. The Thumb insertion (res. 1102–1122) forms a second PCNA contact, contributing to the tripartite interface that makes Pol ε–PCNA resistant to displacement by Pol δ and FEN1.

Function · in vivo

Pol ε performs leading-strand synthesis at the eukaryotic replisome, copying the template 3′→5′ while extending the nascent strand 5′→3′ at >50 nt/s. Base-substitution error rate approaches 10−7 per nucleotide incorporated.

The intrinsic 3′→5′ exonuclease excises misincorporated nucleotides via an intramolecular shuttling mechanism, switching the primer terminus between the polymerase and exonuclease active sites separated by ~40 Å (Roske & Yeeles 2024). The mismatch-recognition pathway proceeds through four structurally resolved states: post-insertion → arrest → frayed substrate → mismatch excision.

Pathogenic hotspots

P286R
EXO · ultra-mutator
Most frequent cancer-associated POLE mutation. Pro→Arg substitution in ExoII motif disrupts the proofreading active site, causing an ultra-mutation phenotype (TMB >100 mut/Mb) in colorectal & endometrial carcinoma.
V411L
EXO · proofreading-deficient
Recurrent exonuclease-domain mutation that abolishes editing capacity. Associated with microsatellite-stable tumours with very high mutational burden, often exceeding 100 mut/Mb.
S297F
EXO · germline / Lynch-like
Germline variant in the ExoI motif; predisposes to polymerase-proofreading-associated polyposis (PPAP). Autosomal dominant with variable penetrance for colorectal adenomas.
F367S
EXO · germline PPAP
Adjacent to ExoIII catalytic residue D368. Germline variant associated with polymerase proofreading-associated polyposis.

Catalytic mechanism

Two-metal-ion catalysis: Metal A (Mg²⁺) lowers the pKa of the primer 3′-OH, generating the nucleophile. Metal B (Mg²⁺) coordinates the β,γ-phosphates of the incoming dNTP and stabilizes the pentacoordinate transition state. Both metals are chelated by conserved aspartate carboxylates (D640, D642, D860).
