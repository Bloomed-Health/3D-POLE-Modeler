Interpretation

Deletion of a single nucleotide at c.138 (within codon 46, CTG→Leu) shifts the reading frame. The new frame encodes 8 aberrant amino acids (Phe-Gly-Val-Glu-Gly-Ser-Phe-Thr) before encountering a premature termination codon (TAG) at what would be residue position 54.

The resulting 53-residue peptide lacks all functional domains: no exonuclease activity, no polymerase activity, no C-terminal zinc-finger platform for holoenzyme assembly. The transcript is predicted to be degraded by nonsense-mediated mRNA decay (NMD), as the PTC is >50 nt upstream of the final exon–exon junction.

In the heterozygous state, the remaining wild-type allele must support all leading-strand synthesis. Complete loss of both alleles would be lethal given Pol ε’s essential role in S-phase DNA replication.

Clinical context

Unlike exonuclease-domain missense mutations (P286R, V411L) that produce a hypermutator phenotype through loss of proofreading with retained polymerase activity, this truncating variant ablates the entire protein.

Heterozygous carriers retain one functional allele. The phenotypic consequence may depend on haploinsufficiency of Pol ε at the replication fork, potentially reducing replicative fidelity or processivity under replication stress.

Clinical interpretation note

Truncating POLE variants (including c.138del) are typically classified as likely benign for polymerase-proofreading-associated polyposis (PPAP). PPAP requires an active but proofreading-deficient polymerase — a null allele does not generate the ultra-mutator phenotype.

Classification per Mur et al., Genome Med 2023.
