pLDDT (predicted Local Distance Difference Test) is a per-residue confidence metric ranging from 0–100. Regions with pLDDT <50 are likely disordered or poorly predicted. Regions >90 are expected to be modeled with high accuracy.

Structure details

This viewer displays the AlphaFold predicted structure for the full-length POLE catalytic subunit (2,286 residues). Unlike experimental PDB structures which capture partial complexes, this model covers the entire polypeptide chain including regions not resolved in cryo-EM.

The default pLDDT coloring highlights regions of high vs. low prediction confidence. Structured catalytic domains (exonuclease, polymerase) typically show high confidence (>90), while flexible loops and disordered termini may show lower scores.

Model note

This is a computational prediction, not an experimentally determined structure. AlphaFold predictions should be interpreted alongside experimental data. For atomic-coordinate analysis from cryo-EM, use the Mol* Coordinate Viewer (PDB 9F6D).
